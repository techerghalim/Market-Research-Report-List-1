<p><p>Fire retardant additives are crucial for enhancing the fire resistance of materials. The market can be divided into two primary types: halogen-free and halogenated additives. Halogen-free fire retardants, made from materials like phosphorus and nitrogen, are environmentally friendly and less toxic. In contrast, halogenated additives, which include compounds containing bromine or chlorine, are highly effective but raise concerns about toxicity and environmental impact. Both types serve various industries, including construction, textiles, and electronics, by improving safety and compliance with regulations.</p></p>
