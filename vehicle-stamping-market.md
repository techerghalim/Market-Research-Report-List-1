<p><p>The vehicle stamping market is segmented into hot stamping and cold stamping. Hot stamping involves shaping metal sheets at elevated temperatures, enhancing formability and allowing for complex geometries while improving strength. It's commonly used for high-performance vehicle components. In contrast, cold stamping operates at room temperature, offering a cost-effective method for producing precise and lightweight parts. It is ideal for mass production of exterior panels and structural components. Both methods are crucial for the automotive industry's efficiency and innovation.</p></p>
