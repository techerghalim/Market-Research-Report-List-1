<p><p>The gas hydrates market is categorized into two main types: onshore gas hydrates and offshore/marine gas hydrates. Onshore gas hydrates are found in terrestrial environments, typically in polar regions or beneath permafrost, where conditions allow methane to form stable hydrate structures. Offshore/marine gas hydrates, on the other hand, occur on the seafloor, particularly in continental margins and near underwater volcanoes. Both types present significant potential for methane extraction, influencing energy markets and environmental research.</p></p>
