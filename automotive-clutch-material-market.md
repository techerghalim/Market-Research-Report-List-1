<p><ul><li>Friction Clutch</li><li>Centrifugal Clutch</li><li>Semi Centrifugal Clutch</li><li>Hydraulic Clutch</li><li>Vacuum Clutch</li><li>Electro-magnetic Clutch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive clutch material market includes several types, each serving specific functions. Friction clutches use friction materials for engagement, while centrifugal clutches activate through centrifugal force, ideal for automatic applications. Semi centrifugal clutches combine both features for varied engagement. Hydraulic clutches utilize fluid pressure for smoother operation, whereas vacuum clutches rely on vacuum pressure for engagement. Lastly, electromagnetic clutches use an electromagnetic field to control engagement, enabling precise and efficient transmission of power, enhancing vehicle performance and drivability.</p></p>
